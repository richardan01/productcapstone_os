# AI Capstone — Discovery Phase
> Course lesson notes | Tags: 4D - Discovery, Week 1, Capstone | Status: Complete

---

## Core Principle

The right problem **drives user and business value**.

Understand your context using the **Business Value Map** — market, business model, and contrast.

---

## 1. Business Value Map

### 1A. Market
- What market does your business need?
- What are the headwinds and tailwinds in the industry?
- What is the growth rate of the market?

### 1B. Business Model
- What growth stage is your business in?
- How does your business make money?
- Is the business B2B, B2C, or B2G?

### 1C. Contrast (Blue Ocean Canvas)
- What makes you different? Use the Blue Ocean Canvas → **directly shapes product strategy**
  - Which activities to pursue differently
  - What to eliminate or stop doing
  - What to reduce/raise (scale back or double down)

---

## 2. Feature Value Map (Enhancing existing features with AI)
- Who are the customers/buyers of your product?
- Who is the most important user?
- What features/services do you provide — core features and how they address user needs?

---

## 3. User Value Map

### 3A. Map the User Journey
1. Identify the highest revenue-impacting persona
2. Break down the high-level stages of the journey they follow
3. List activities they do in each stage (what do they do, think, feel)

### 3B. Pain Points
1. Identify pain points, challenges, gaps, or friction points
2. List all pain points

### 3C. Suitability of AI to Solve the Pain Point
1. Map pain points with an intelligence processor (AI): **Input → Output**
2. Identify the pattern:
   - **Scaling Pattern** — doing more of what a human would do
   - **Translation Pattern** — converting one form to another
   - **Consistency Pattern** — enforcing standards at scale
   - **Expertise Pattern** — applying specialized knowledge
   - **Combination Pattern** — mixing multiple patterns
3. Prioritize pain points and rank by **frequency × severity**
   - Higher frequency + higher severity = more likely the user will adopt your AI solution

---

## 4. Developing an AI Solution Hypothesis

### Diverge — Ideate
- "How Might We..." (HMW) exercises

### Converge — Prioritize
Evaluate each idea on two axes:
- **Impact to business & user (Why)**:
  - High impact, impossible to build → needs research
  - Low impact, easy to build → toy
- **Technical feasibility (How)**

### Articulate Your AI Opportunity Statement
> Our business operates in \<industry\> and creates value by \<unique value proposition\>. To deliver this value, our product offers \<features/services\> that meet the needs of \<customers/users\>. These customers are primarily \<target persona\>, who experience \<key pain points\> as they try to achieve \<user goal\>. To address these pain points, we propose an AI-powered solution that \<solution\>, delivering value to \<beneficiaries\>.
